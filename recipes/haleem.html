Haleem

What is Haleem?

Haleem is a rich and hearty dish that is popular in the Indian subcontinent, particularly in Pakistan, India, and Bangladesh. It is a slow-cooked stew made with a combination of lentils, meat (usually beef or mutton), and various grains, resulting in a thick and flavorful consistency.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed lentils (a combination of wheat, barley, and gram lentils)
  • 500 grams of boneless beef or mutton, cut into small pieces
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2-inch piece of ginger, grated or finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons of red chili powder (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon powder
  • 4-5 cups of water or beef/mutton broth
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • For garnish: fried onions, chopped coriander leaves, ginger slices, lemon wedges

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak mixed lentils (wheat, barley, and gram lentils) for 4-6 hours or overnight. Drain before cooking.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pot or pressure cooker over medium heat.
  3. Saut√© sliced onions until golden brown.
  4. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, cook for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add meat (beef or mutton) and cook until lightly browned.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, cinnamon powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Pour in water or beef/mutton broth, ensuring meat is submerged.
  8. Pressure cooker: Cook on high pressure for 25-30 minutes. Pot: Simmer on low heat for 3-4 hours, stirring occasionally.
  9. Mash and stir the mixture until it reaches a smooth and porridge-like consistency.
  10. Adjust seasoning if needed.
  11. In a separate pan, fry additional sliced onions until crispy and golden brown.
  12. Serve hot, garnished with fried onions, chopped coriander leaves, ginger slices, and lemon juice.
  13. Enjoy with naan bread or roti.
